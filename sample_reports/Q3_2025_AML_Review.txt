INTERNAL AUDIT REPORT
Report Reference: IA-2025-Q3-AML-001
Date: September 30, 2025
Region: Europe
Audit Type: AML Transaction Monitoring Compliance
Severity Classification: Medium
 
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
The Q3 2025 AML compliance review covered transaction monitoring processes across
the European region. Overall compliance rate reached 94%, an improvement of 6%
from Q2 2025. Two medium findings and one low finding were raised.
 
FINDING 1: MEDIUM — ALERT REVIEW BACKLOG
Finding Title: AML Alert Backlog Exceeding 30-Day Review Threshold
Severity: Medium
Region: Europe
Responsible Party: Compliance Operations — Marie Dubois
Deadline: November 30, 2025
Description: 156 AML transaction monitoring alerts had not been reviewed within
the mandatory 30-day window. The backlog accumulation was concentrated in
the French and German portfolios.
Root Cause: Staff attrition of 2 compliance analysts in Q3.
Recommendation: Prioritise alert review using risk-based scoring. Hire 1 analyst.
 
FINDING 2: LOW — DOCUMENTATION QUALITY
Finding Title: Inconsistent SAR Filing Documentation
Severity: Low
Region: Europe
Responsible Party: Compliance — Jean-Pierre Moreau
Deadline: March 31, 2026
Description: Review of 50 Suspicious Activity Reports found inconsistent
documentation standards across teams. 20% lacked sufficient narrative quality.
Recommendation: Implement SAR writing training and quality checklist.
